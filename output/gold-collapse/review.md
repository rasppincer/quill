Feel free to push back on these - review is by Gemini

Structural Issues

    Pacing (The "Crash"): The transition from the discovery phase to the market collapse is handled primarily through a series of diary-style entries and stats. While this establishes a timeline, it loses the tension of the Кървавият петък (bloody Friday). Consider dramatizing one of these days.
    Resolution: The Epilogue feels rushed. Moving from "SEC witness" to "back to gaming" in a few paragraphs glosses over the consequences of his actions. The transition feels like a summary of a longer ending rather than the ending itself.

Oddities & Immersion

    Access to Power: The biggest suspension-of-disbelief hurdle is Daniel’s rapid access to high-level figures. A solitary trader in Mladost 4 reaching an LMBA director in London via a casual contact (Nikolov) feels too streamlined. It weakens the stakes; the process of "breaking into" this elite circle could provide significant tension.
    The "Technobabble": The "Quantum Digital Bridge" is highly abstract. While it doesn't need to be fully explained, adding one or two grounding details about why the bridge exists (e.g., a specific, slightly sinister motivation from the researchers beyond just "control") would make the plot feel less like a deus ex machina.

Suggestions

    "Show, Don't Tell": The explanation of how the bot farms work is very "data-dump" heavy. Instead of Daniel explaining the math, show a snippet of a Bilibili stream where the chat is going wild.
    Dialogue: The dialogue during the London meeting with Hughes is a bit functional/stiff. It serves to dump information rather than reveal character. Try to make these characters more skeptical or antagonistic to increase the friction.
    Mladost 4 Setting: You have a great setting in Mladost 4. Use it more to contrast with the high-stakes global market. Describe the sounds of the neighborhood, the specific light coming into his apartment, or the contrast between his humble physical surroundings and his massive digital influence.

Domain Accuracy

    PoE 2 Mechanics: The use of "stash" and "Divine Orbs" as currency is accurate and authentic to the gaming community. Note: In the actual Path of Exile game, "Gold" is not the primary trading currency (usually Chaos/Divine Orbs are). While this is a fictionalized version, specifying why Gold has been introduced in the game would show in depth domain knowledge.
    Trading Logic: The logic of shorting gold and longing mining stocks is sound - this is standard contrarian trading behavior.
    Bulgarian Language: The language is natural, authentic, and evocative. The names and cultural references feel grounded and local.

Continuity

    Timeline: The 6-week diary progression is clear and tracks well.
    Character: Daniel’s transition from "observer" to "participant" is a strong arc, but be careful not to make him too capable too quickly. The speed at which he goes from "guy on a laptop" to "witness for the SEC" is very fast. Ensure he shows at least a little bit of anxiety or hesitation about entering the world of high-finance regulatory bodies.
